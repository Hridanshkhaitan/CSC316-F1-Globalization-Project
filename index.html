F1'S RAPID EXPANSION

BRIEF OVERVIEW

  • F1 used to be primarily European with 62% of races in 2016 being held in Europe.
  • In 2017, Liberty Media purchased Formula 1. They've expanded F1 into a much more global phenomenon
  • Europe only hosts 41% of the F1 races as of 2024.

WITH GLOBALIZATION COME SOME PROBLEMS

Unfortunately, ticket prices for attending F1 races have gone up significantly. While a lot of race video is available online and live races are accessible through streaming services and cable networks, the in-person experience is hard to replicate for many fans.

Drive to Survive's Lasting Impact on Formula 1

KEY IMPACTS OF DRIVE TO SURVIVE
  • Accelerated Global Expansion: Attracted new fans across North America, Asia, and other emerging markets. 53% of new American F1 fans mention that DTS is part of the reason they started watching the sport.
  • Younger & Diverse Viewers: Significant uptick in affluent fans under 35, boosting F1’s cultural relevance.

The Las Vegas Grand Prix was one of the most significant investments in motorsport history, with Formula 1 and local stakeholders investing hundreds of millions of dollars into the event’s infrastructure, marketing, and logistics. The gamble paid off tremendously, with the race generating an estimated $1.5 billion in total economic impact for Las Vegas in 2023. This figure outperformed even Super Bowl LVIII, which had an estimated economic impact of around $1 billion. The Grand Prix brought in hundreds of thousands of visitors, drove record-breaking hotel occupancy rates, and boosted spending across casinos, restaurants, and entertainment venues, solidifying its status as a premier global sporting event with a lasting economic footprint.
